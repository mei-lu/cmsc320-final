Background¶

Webtoons are a type of digital comic that originated in South Korea usually meant to be read on computers and smartphones. Webtoons usually feature a few of common traits: each episode is published on one long, vertical strip (making use of an infinite canvas rather than multiple pages so that it is easier to read on a smartphone or computer); some will feature music and animations that play during each chapter; and unlike the majority of East Asian comics, they will most likely be in color rather than black-and-white since they are published digitally on a website or app rather than physically in a magazine.

Source

Motivation¶

I have always been a fan of reading comics and Webtoons since I was a kid. Nowadays with the explosion of Webtoon on a international scale, they have become so popular that TV adaptations of them have come out. I would like to find out what elements make a webtoon series successful and amass millions of readers. Is there a special formula? Is it pure luck? What does it take to become a top webtoon? Let's find out what makes a webtoon series popular in current times by analyzing some data scraped from the Webtoons site.

Number Formatting¶

Like and subscriber counts sometimes abbreviate millions into the symbol 'M', which will not be helpful when we want to make calculations with it. Let's convert all of these text representations in the table into numerical values!

We must also convert these columns from string to numerical types. In the process, commas are removed so the convertion can happen smoothly.

In [ ]:
# We can make a function since we have to apply this both to the subscribe column and the likes column
print("Before Conversion:")
print(dataset['subscribe'].head())
print(dataset['likes'].head())
def value_to_int(x):
    # Depending on the ending of the number, append the correct amount of zeroes
    if 'K' in x:
        if len(x) > 1:
            return int(float(x.replace('K', '')) * 1000)
        return 1000
    if 'M' in x:
        if len(x) > 1:
            return int(float(x.replace('M', '')) * 1000000)
        return 1000000
    if 'B' in x:
        return float(x.replace('B', '')) * 1000000000
    return x

# Remove all commas from the numerical columns
dataset['subscribe'] = dataset['subscribe'].replace(',','', regex=True)
dataset['likes'] = dataset['likes'].replace(',','', regex=True)
dataset['episodes'] = dataset['episodes'].replace(',','', regex=True)

dataset['subscribe'] = dataset['subscribe'].apply(value_to_int)
dataset['likes'] = dataset['likes'].apply(value_to_int)

dataset['likes'] = pd.to_numeric(pd.Series(dataset.likes))

Why Focus on Subscribership?¶

The above visualizations I have chosen all have to do with subscriber count (with the exception of one). When a reader subscribes to a story, indicating some sort of investment in it. Subscriptions matter more than views, since the website and application can choose to promote a webtoon, yet it's not an indicator of it having lasting popularity.

Subscribers can accumulate over time, thus this metric could bias towards older Webtoons. To mitigate this issue, I decided to also visualize a lot of the data by year to see a more accurate representation of the popular webtoons both of all time and recently. We can also compare webtoons published in the same year to have a better comparison between their subscriber counts. I chose to use the Median as a measure that isn't as affected by skew as the mean, to account for certain series that may be outliers and explode in popularity compared to the other webtoons.

Forming Our Hypothesis¶

Does Release Year Matter?¶

Popular webtoons are spread across different release dates. Being an older webtoon does not mean the series will be more popular. Instead, webtoons can get popular both over time and after being recently released. Don't expect a webtoon released within the current to last year to shoot into the charts though, the chances are more slim.

Does Genre Matter?¶

Focusing on the top 5 most popular genres on the site reflects the most popular genres in the top subscribed webtoons. To help a webtoon be more popular, it should be about genres that readers care about.

Does Rating Matter?¶

Definitely! In order to have a webtoon that people want to subscribe to, the ratings will need to be high. People are only willing to subscribe to webtoons that are highly regarded.

Does Number of Episodes Matter?¶

Not particularly, some popular webtoons are long running (1000+), while most stay in the 0-200 episode range. However, top 100 popular webtoons have a median episode length of 82, which is almost double than the median length across all webtoons at 42.

Does Having a Hit Webtoon Already Matter?¶

It can be good, but it's not a requirement. Only a small amount of authors in the top 100 subscribed webtoons list have another webtoon.

Part 5: Insights and Conclusion¶

Conclusion¶

Unfortunately, there is no magic formula to make a webtoon successful. The common elements we identified in the top 100 most subscribed webtoons such as having a high rating, but that is not reflective throughout the webtoon website. The good thing is though, we found out that a great webtoon does not necessarily need to be older to attain success - newer ones can still become a hit! If you're thinking about starting a webtoon series, success may come to you sooner than you think. Higher ratings and longer length does correlate positively, but not by much. This makes sense intuitively since people want to subscribe to webtoons that they think are good and it can take time to accumulate subscribers. Thus the best thing a webtoon author can do is to write an outstanding story and be consistent with his/her work. Hopefully, their work will be a hit!

It's difficult to tell from data to predict the success of a webtoon - after all there are so many elements unable to be described by emperical data. Art is quite subjective after all!

Pros and Cons of Approach¶

Pros: The data did not require much clean up and contained most of the key information about the webtoons that are publically available. We could identify a metric that indicated popularity via subsribership and compare it to various criteria. We also had unique values for each series and did not have to deal with missing values.

Cons: The data does not contain information about which webtoons were promoted by the site on the homepage banner. This could affect the popularity of a webtoon as there would be more visibility to the userbase. Other things not accounted along these lines are adventisements the author paid for or Webtoon.com displayed on other sites.
